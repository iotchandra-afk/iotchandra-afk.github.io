Profile

Chandra Kanojia

Enterprise Transformation Executive

Chandra Kanojia is an enterprise transformation executive who has led inside a Fortune 100 operating environment and advised large organizations through global transformation. He is most effective when technology investment has outpaced the operating model and the business needs a clearer line from strategy to changed work and measurable value. His leadership brings business, technology, operations, governance, and change leaders around the same operating problem, then stays with the work until the constraint moves and the result is visible.

Leadership focus

Enterprise transformation and operating models

Align executive intent, operating-model change, technology delivery, governance, and adoption around measurable business outcomes.

Platforms, workflow, and service operations

Turn CRM, workflow, analytics, and enterprise platforms into systems that improve how work is prioritized, routed, decided, and completed.

Governed AI and value realization

Connect use-case selection, data context, control boundaries, workflow fit, operating readiness, and adoption so AI can move toward repeatable enterprise use.

Selected enterprise impact

Enterprise service and capacity

In a large regulated service environment, Chandra connected workflow, CRM, analytics, reliability, governance, and adoption around how work moved. The leadership pattern was to treat capacity, service quality, risk, and experience as one operating problem rather than separate technology programs.

Outcome

Greater visibility, more consistent execution, and more room for higher-value work.

Selected enterprise impact

Global transformation execution

Across a major multi-country transformation, Chandra connected process design, business architecture, platform capability, governance, and regional delivery. The work balanced common direction with local execution across complex markets and operating conditions.

Outcome

Simpler workflows, faster response, and measurable productivity and cost improvement.

Selected enterprise impact

Platforms and governed intelligence

Across enterprise platforms and AI-enabled workflows, Chandra aligned product, engineering, data, operations, governance, and change around the business outcome. The emphasis remained on trusted context, operating readiness, accountability, and whether the capability changed the work.

Outcome

Stronger reliability, clearer ownership, and a more disciplined path from delivery to operating value.

Career arc

Operating leadership informed by global consulting.

His career includes leadership roles at Fidelity Investments, Accenture, Cognizant, and Virtusa. Across operating and consulting environments, he has led enterprise platforms, digital service, CRM and workflow modernization, business architecture, and global transformation across financial services, insurance, healthcare, and legal services.

Chandra has hosted and spoken in executive, academic, and industry forums on AI-enabled customer experience, digital cooperation, healthcare, sustainability, and the circular economy. Selected recordings and conversations appear in Perspectives.

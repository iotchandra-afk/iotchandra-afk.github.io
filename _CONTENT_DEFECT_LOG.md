# Content Defect Log
## Chandra Kanojia Executive Site — Copy Audit
Audited: May 2025

---

## Summary

17 defects identified across 4 pages. Primary failure mode: copy that describes what the
page is trying to be instead of stating Chandra's work directly. Secondary failure mode:
third-person positioning language that reads as a strategy memo about Chandra rather than
Chandra's own executive record. One defect on the insights page (section heading).
Contact page is clean.

---

## Defects by Page

### EXECUTIVE PROFILE — 8 defects

| # | Current Phrase | Issue | Replacement |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | "An authoritative record of enterprise transformation leadership. Not a chronological resume — a thematic account of operating scope, institutional context, and measurable outcomes." | Describes what the page is trying to be. Self-conscious meta-language. "Not a chronological resume" is defensive. "Thematic account" and "institutional context" are academic. | "The work is simple to state and hard to execute: help complex enterprises modernize how they operate, make AI and platform change usable at scale, and turn strategy into measurable business outcomes." |
| 2 | "Organized by operating theme — not by employer or title sequence." | Same meta-language problem. Explains page structure to the reader instead of leading with content. | Remove entirely. The content speaks for itself. |
| 3 | "His core operating lane is specific:" | Strategy-document phrasing. "Core operating lane" is a brand positioning term, not executive voice. | "His focus is specific:" |
| 4 | "Chandra's positioning: the executive who owns the outcome. Not an advisor. Not a theorist. Not a vendor consultant. The operator who has already solved the problems the organization faces." | This is positioning copy about Chandra written in the voice of a brand strategist. The "Not X. Not Y." construction is a marketing device. Reads as generated. | "He owns the outcome — not as an advisor to it, but as the executive accountable for it." |
| 5 | "The scale of this engagement breadth is a direct competitive advantage: Chandra has operated inside the governance frameworks, legacy infrastructure, and organizational dynamics that most transformation leaders have only studied from the outside." | Consultant brochure language. "Direct competitive advantage" and "studied from the outside" are pitch-deck phrases. | "Most executives in this space studied these environments. Chandra operated inside them." |
| 6 | "Global delivery at this scale is not primarily a logistics challenge. It is a governance and accountability design problem. Chandra's record in multi-jurisdictional environments reflects an operating approach that scales without losing coherence." | The last sentence is vague and corporate. "Scales without losing coherence" means nothing specific. | "Global delivery at this scale is a governance problem before it is a logistics problem. Getting the decision structure right across jurisdictions determines whether the program holds together." |
| 7 | "The credential combination reflects the full span of Chandra's operating scope: engineering foundations from NIT Allahabad, executive business leadership from IIM Bangalore, and CIO-level technology governance from Carnegie Mellon." | "Credential combination reflects the full span of operating scope" is a strategy-document phrase. The explanation is redundant — the credentials speak for themselves. | "Engineering at NIT Allahabad. Business leadership at IIM Bangalore. CIO-level program at Carnegie Mellon." Let them stand without explanation. |
| 8 | "This is not a general management profile. It is a profile built specifically for the CIO/CDO/CAIO operating mandate." | "A profile built specifically for" is a sales pitch. The reader draws their own conclusion. | Remove entirely. |

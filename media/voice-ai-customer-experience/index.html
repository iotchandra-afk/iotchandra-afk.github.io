Voice Is the Next Frontier of User Experience

How voice and AI can make customer interactions faster, more contextual, and more natural while supporting secure service.

The argument

Voice changes the interaction model because people can speak faster than they type and can ask for help in the moment. AI provides the intelligence behind the interaction. Information-based service questions are an early fit, while voice biometrics and device context can support stronger security for more sensitive transactions.

The objective is not voice for novelty. It is a more natural relationship with the customer, delivered in context and at the speed the customer expects.

EDITED TRANSCRIPT

How do we engage with our customers in a time-poor world? Voice has the capacity to answer in the moment. While our average typing speed is about 40 words per minute, we can speak about 200 words in the same minute. Voice is the new user experience. With voice, businesses can connect with customers in the moment and in the context they are in. Behind voice is the power of AI that can answer demands at the speed customers prefer. Voice-based interactions can bridge the frustrating gap that typing creates. Information-based service interactions are especially suited to voice assistants. For more secure transactions, voice biometrics and device location can help deliver a frictionless but secure experience. The opportunity is to build stronger customer relationships and brand loyalty by communicating in the customer’s language, on their terms, and at the speed they want.

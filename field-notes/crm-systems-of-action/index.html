The CRM Trap: When Systems of Record Never Become Systems of Action

A platform that records the work but does not improve how the work moves leaves operating value trapped.

Chandra KanojiaPublished July 5, 2026 · 8 min read

CRM programs often succeed at centralizing records and still disappoint the people doing the work. The platform contains more information, but employees continue to search across systems, reconstruct context, manage handoffs outside the workflow, and rely on local knowledge to decide what happens next.

The system became a better record. It did not become a better operating system.

Recording activity is not guiding action

A system of record answers what happened. A system of action helps determine what should happen next, who should act, what context is required, which controls apply, and how the outcome will be measured.

That shift requires more than a new interface or automation layer. It requires workflow, decision, data, and operating-model design.

The hidden work around CRM

Employees compensate for weak workflow design with spreadsheets, notes, inboxes, side conversations, and memory. Those workarounds are difficult to govern and nearly impossible to scale. They also hide the true cost of the operating model.

The organization may believe the process is standardized because the final activity is recorded. The work that produced the activity remains fragmented.

Build context into the flow

A system of action assembles the relevant customer, case, relationship, policy, interaction, and risk context at the point of work. It reduces the need to search and reconcile. It makes ownership and status visible. It supports consistent decisions while preserving room for expert judgment.

Use AI where it changes the flow

AI can summarize history, prepare meetings, classify work, recommend routing, draft follow-up, identify risk, or suggest the next best action. These capabilities create more value when they are embedded in the workflow and tied to decision boundaries, evidence, and accountability.

Adding a conversational interface to fragmented work does not solve the fragmentation.

Measure operating outcomes

CRM success should be visible in the economics and experience of the work: lower effort, fewer handoffs, better throughput, clearer ownership, improved customer continuity, stronger reliability, and greater capacity for high-value interactions.

Adoption also becomes more meaningful. People use the platform because it helps them perform, not because compliance requires another field to be completed.

The leadership test

Ask whether the platform is merely documenting the current operating model or actively improving it. If the most important decisions and handoffs still happen outside the system, operating value remains trapped.

The goal is not a more complete record of work. It is a better way for work to move.

When this becomes relevant

Use this argument when the organization is producing transformation activity but cannot yet show that the operating constraint, decision model, or economics of work have changed.
